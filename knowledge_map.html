01
Core Macro Frameworks — Gold
▼
Two-channel gold model — fear/geopolitics channel (war premium, CB buying) and inflation chain channel (oil → CPI → Fed → yields → DXY → gold)
Four-step headline framework — Step 0 expectation theory, Step 1 real yields (DFII10 + T10YIE), Step 2 Fed channel (hike/cut probability), Step 3 fear channel (war premium). Channels aligned = trade, channels fighting = wait.
Business cycle four phases — Phase 1 recovery, Phase 2 expansion, Phase 3 late cycle (GDP slowing, CPI sticky, Fed paused), Phase 4 recession (forced cuts). Gold most bullish in late Phase 3 loading into Phase 4.
Five regime variables — Fed reaction function, DXY direction, real yields level, geopolitical premium size, Western ETF flows. All five flipped bearish when Warsh announced January 29 2026.
Stagflation trap — economy too weak to justify hiking, inflation too high to justify cutting. Fed frozen. Gold in a holding pattern until one side breaks.
Expectation theory — the move is always relative to what was already priced. A hot print is bearish only if it beats consensus. Size of the surprise determines size of the move.
Historical analogs — 1973 OPEC oil shock (supply shock + inflation + forced hike), 2007-09 GFC (Phase 3→4, gold $651→$1,911), 2019-20 COVID (Phase 4 flush -14%, QE, gold +40% in 5 months)

Real Yield Framework
▼
Core formula
Real yield = Nominal (DGS10) − Breakeven (T10YIE) = DFII10 · This is the primary ceiling on gold
TradingView — TVC:US10Y minus FRED:T10YIE for real-time real yield. Open every morning before looking at price.
Key levels — above +2% headwind, near 0% bullish, negative explosive. Current: +2.17% = active ceiling.
Decomposition rule — if nominals rise faster than breakevens: real yields rise, bearish. If breakevens rise faster than nominals: real yields fall, bullish. Breakevens rising is gold's friend.
Correction learned — breakevens rising compresses real yields (bullish). Initial analysis had this backwards. Nominals rising is the bearish driver.
What compresses the ceiling — deal confirmed + oil below $85 → CPI falls → Fed cuts → real yields compress → gold launches.

Trading Framework
▼
A1 definition — score 6+ pts AND 2+ core T1 AND 1+ T2 AND all 6 hard rules confirmed. Both conditions simultaneously. Missing either = not A1.
Six hard rules — not within 10m of 9:30am · valid window 6am-2pm or 7-9pm · not Sunday night · no major event within 2h · under 2 trades · at desk and focused
Level validity — Right away: confluences stacked + impulsive approach + fresh level (1-2nd touch) + 2+ correlators in sync. Wait: single confluence, slow grind 3+ touches, mid-session dead zone. Discard: broke through, 5th+ touch, middle of range, HTF opposing.
Entry types — right away (with trend, A1) · wait for sweep (against trend, slow grind) · wait for retest (missed breakout) · wait for 15m close (data/news day)
Stop types — tight structural (with trend, A1, clear level) · wide swing zone (against trend, major KL, beyond sweep low not the KL itself)
38 fib max rule — non-negotiable ceiling on TP when buying dip against recent trend. Not a guideline — a rule.
BE rule — move SL to BE only if structure actually breaks. Always take small partials before moving. Never move BE without reducing.
Against-trend requirements — sweep confirmed + divergence present + correlators at session extremity + overextension visible. All four needed.
09
Market Profile and Technical Analysis
▼
Day types — trend day, range day, neutral day, news volatile day. Identified from profile structure before the session opens. Changes the entire playbook.
Open types — open drive (directional, right away valid) · open test drive (tests prior level then drives) · open rejection reverse · open auction (balanced, no direction)
POC — Point of Control. Gold at POC = equilibrium. Below POC before open = bearish value. Above = bullish.
Value area — VAH and VAL containing 70% of volume. Break outside VA on open = potential trend day. Rejection at VA boundary = range day.
LVN — Low Volume Node. Thin area where price moves fast. Single most important confluence for right away entries. Mark these on every profile.
Range day rule — no HVA trades. Only extremity fades at VAH and VAL. Do not enter in the middle of the range.
Technical prep daily — Major KL H4, trendlines H4-H1, patterns HTF-LTF, fibs, market profile. Must be done before the session. Not optional.

Mistakes Identified — What Was Built to Fix Each
▼
SL too tight
Tight stop on swing zone or against-trend entry — stopped out by normal noise before the trade played out.
System built
Wide vs tight framework. Wide when: swing zone, major KL, volatile day, against trend. SL beyond sweep low not the KL itself.
Rule now
Stop type selected before entry as part of checklist. Context dictates the stop.
Entry not at extremity
Entering in the middle of the range or before price reached the confluent level.
System built
Level validity inspection. Middle of range is a hard discard. Extremity required. Confluences must be stacked at the level not near it.
Rule now
Not middle of range is a hard rule chip. Checklist will not score A1 without valid level at extremity.
Not taking profits
Holding past natural target. Moving to BE without taking partials first.
System built
Small TP mandatory for counter-trend and momentum. BE rule: always take partials before moving stop.
Rule now
38 fib max against trend is non-negotiable. Partial scale is a selectable target type. TP defined before entry.
Not pulling trigger
Seeing valid A1 setup, confirming all confluences, then freezing at execution.
System built
Process replaces decision. A1 checklist score means execute. Three pre-trade questions answered = enter. No additional confirmation allowed.
Rule now
0.01 lots eliminated. Meaningful size only. More reps at small but real size until execution is automatic.
Shorting into catalyst
BTC short on MoU weekend. Shorting highest beta risk asset into most risk-on catalyst of the year.
System built
Never short the initial move of a confirmed catalyst. Wait for bond market to push back first. It always signals before equities crack.
Rule now
Know which ceiling mechanism exists. BTC has none. Gold has real yields. Never short pure sentiment into confirmed risk-on.
Treating approach as short signal
Considering gold short because price was near the 200-day EMA before confirmed rejection.
System built
Approach is not the signal. Touch is not the signal. 15-minute close back below after confirmed rejection is the signal.
Rule now
Resistance is only tradeable after rejection is confirmed — not anticipated.
